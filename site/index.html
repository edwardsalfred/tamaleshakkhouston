The Story

From the Delta. By the family. To Houston.

Twenty years ago, Katrina Thompson moved from Greenville, Mississippi to Houston and started missing the tamales she grew up on. There is no real substitute. Mexican tamales are a different food. Her brother Van had been quietly perfecting a hot tamale recipe back home, and in his first year on the festival circuit, he won the home division of the legendary Delta Hot Tamale Festival.

He went pro. Two Grand Championships later, in 2021, Katrina and Van partnered to bring the Shakk to Houston. Van still rolls the batches in Greenville. They are frozen, packed, and driven west to Pearland.

"I have some people, once they find us, who say oh my god, we have been looking for a Mississippi Delta tamale." Katrina Thompson, co-founder

The Delta Way

If it is not cornmeal and cigar-shaped, it is not a Delta tamale.

01

Cornmeal, not masa

Delta dough is ground cornmeal. Sandier texture. Holds spice better. Toasts at the edges where it meets the cooking liquid.

02

Cigar-rolled

Smaller and tighter than the Mexican version. Two bites in, two out. Built for stacking in a paper sack and eating in your truck.

03

Seasoned through

The seasoning is in the dough, not just the meat. Every layer is hot. Van guards the blend. Even Katrina does not know the full formula.

04

Simmered, not steamed

Partially submerged in a spiced cooking liquid. That liquid gets ladled back over the tamale at service. That is the Delta finish.
