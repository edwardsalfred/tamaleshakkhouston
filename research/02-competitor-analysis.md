## Patterns of the Top 10%

After scoring all five, three patterns separate the standouts (Alamo, Balderas) from the rest:

1. **Story before menu.** Both top scorers lead with a founder narrative (Balderas's military background, Alamo's "Houston's Famous"), THEN show the food. Generic competitors lead with food and never explain why. Tamale Shakk has a stronger story than any of them: festival champions, family operation, real Mississippi production, freeze-and-haul logistics. **This is the biggest single opportunity.**

2. **Real online ordering, not a phone number.** Three of five had Toast Tab, Square, or BestFoodTrucks integration. The current Tamale Shakk site has only "for hours of operation, call us." This is a 2026 floor, not a ceiling. Even if pre-launch we keep it as a "text to pre-order" lane, the homepage CTA must look like a button, not a phone number.

3. **Visible social proof above the fold.** Alamo's 4.5-star Google rating and embedded testimonials carry their conversion. None of the competitors had press quotes pulled out as design elements. but Tamale Shakk has been profiled in *Above the Fold* (Substack food publication) AND has the four Delta Festival championship trophies. Both belong on the homepage.

## Patterns of the bottom

- Everything-is-on-the-homepage Wix templates with no IA
- "Coming Soon" or "Call us for hours" as the only schedule signal
- Phone numbers as the only CTA (Tamale Shakk's current state)
- Black or muddy backgrounds with low-contrast text
- Stock food photography that doesn't match the actual product

## Visual identity benchmarks

Across the comp set, the winning palettes used:
- Warm, food-forward neutrals (cream, terracotta, deep walnut)
- One saturated accent (Alamo: red; Balderas: golden yellow; Doe's: deep brown)
- Typography paired one display serif/slab with one neutral sans

**Tamale Shakk is already there in their physical brand**. burnt orange truck, red+gold logo, brown wooden shack motif. The website just needs to honor what's already painted on the side of the trailer.

## SEO landscape

Top organic queries in the niche: "best tamales houston", "delta tamales houston", "mississippi tamales texas", "hot tamales near me", "houston food truck tamales", "tamale catering pearland". Of these, **"delta tamales houston" is essentially uncontested**. Tamale Shakk is the only operator that actually sells Mississippi Delta-style tamales in Houston, and their existing site does nothing to claim that term. Easy wins:
- Page title tag with "Mississippi Delta Tamales" + "Houston"
- H1 with the same phrase
- A "What is a Delta Tamale" educational section (also doubles as Schema FAQ)
- Schema markup for FoodEstablishment + LocalBusiness with "Pearland, TX" service area
- Alt text on hero image with the keyword
