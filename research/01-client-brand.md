## Why the existing site fails
The current tamaleshakkhouston.com is a Wix one-pager: logo, two phone numbers, "For hours of operation call us." That's it. Their actual story (Greenville roots, Van's recipe, Delta Festival domination, the freeze-and-haul logistics) is one of the best small-business narratives in Texas food, and the site says none of it. There is no menu, no schedule, no online ordering, no founder photo, no press quotes. Massive upside on rebuild.

## Founder & Origin Story (from press research)
- **Katrina Thompson** moved from Greenville, Mississippi to Houston ~20 years ago. Missed the tamales of her childhood.
- Her brother **Van** spent ~a decade in Greenville perfecting a hot tamale recipe. Won "home hots" division of the Delta Hot Tamale Festival in his first year. Went pro and launched The Tamale Shakk in MS.
- 2018 + 2021: **Grand Champions of the Delta Hot Tamale Festival** (the festival heavyweight title)
- 2021: Katrina, encouraged by her husband, partnered with Van to bring the Shakk to Houston
- Production model: Van makes large batches in Greenville, freezes, and they're transported to Houston for sale via the food truck
- Van still guards the seasoning blend. even Katrina doesn't know the full formula as of 2024
- Quote (Katrina): "I have some people, once they find us, who say 'oh my god, we've been looking for a Mississippi Delta tamale.'"
- Quote (Van): "I know in order for things to grow, I can't grow it all by myself."

## What Makes Delta Tamales Different (key copy hook)
- **Cornmeal dough**, not masa
- **Cigar-like shape**, smaller than Mexican tamales
- **Seasoning permeates dough AND filling** (not just the meat)
- **Simmered partially submerged** in fiery cooking liquid. that liquid is served over the tamales
- This is a completely different food category from Tex-Mex tamales sold elsewhere in Houston
